Emily Mandamin is an Anishinaabe basketball player from Iskatewizaagegan 39 Independent First Nation (Shoal Lake #39) in northwestern Ontario. She is affiliated with the Caribou Clan and bears the traditional name Giiwedin Giizis, meaning "Northern Sun".

Early Life and Education

At the age of 12, Mandamin committed to pursuing high-level basketball, a decision that led her to leave her home community. She received 13 scholarship offers from institutions in the United States and Canada, ultimately accepting a full-ride scholarship during the pandemic. She played for women's college basketball teams in Illinois, Kansas, and Texas. In the summer of 2023, Mandamin committed to the University of Manitoba Bisons, marking a return to Winnipeg, where she had moved in her teens to further her basketball career.

College Career

As a member of the Manitoba Bisons women's basketball team, Mandamin has been recognized for her contributions on the court. In a December 2024 game against the University of Winnipeg, she scored 11 points and achieved a career-high 11 rebounds.

By January 2025, she led the team with 20 three-pointers for the season.

Advocacy and Community Engagement

Mandamin is deeply connected to her Indigenous heritage, participating in traditional ceremonies such as the Sundance and Jingle Dress dancing at powwows. She views sport as medicine and actively contributes to shaping the next generation by participating in coaching clinics, engaging with communities, and speaking at conferences. She has also worked to refurbish the basketball court in her home community, emphasizing the importance of providing every child a place to play.

Recognition

In 2023, Mandamin was featured in a partnership between the Toronto Raptors and the Toronto Transit Commission to celebrate Indigenous athletes' contributions to Canadian sport. Her image was displayed on buses and streetcars in Toronto as part of this initiative.

Personal Philosophy

Mandamin has stated, "Basketball has saved my life so many times," highlighting the sport's significance in her personal development and as a tool for navigating life's challenges.
